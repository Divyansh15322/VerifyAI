VerifyAI Healthcare Evidence Verification Policy Manual
Document Code: HC-POL-2026-V1
Last Updated: January 15, 2026

1. INTRODUCTION & SCOPE
This document outlines compliance criteria for healthcare claim reimbursement, patient prescription audits, and clinical treatment validations. 

2. CRITICAL PRE-REQUISITES (MUST Haves)
For any medical reimbursement claim to be statused as "Supported", the uploaded files MUST contain the following visual and textual indicators:
- Clinical Letterhead: The name, address, and contact details of the clinic or hospital must be printed clearly at the top.
- Practitioner License Number: The medical practitioner's registry number (e.g. Reg #MD-XXXXXX or Lic #XX-XXXXX) must be present on the prescription.
- Date of Treatment: Must align with the claimant statement, falling within the policy coverage timeframe.
- Signature / Stamp: A physical or digital signature of the physician or dentist, along with the clinic's official stamp, is strictly required.

3. VALIDATION PROTOCOLS
- Prescription Audits: AI agents must cross-reference prescribed drugs and dosages with the patient diagnosis description. Inconsistencies must flag the claim as "Needs Review".
- Dental Extract Reimbursements: Standard dental tooth extractions are subject to standard code check lists. An itemized invoice must accompany the dentist prescription.

4. EXCLUSIONS & PENALTIES
- Self-prescribed drugs or letters missing doctor registration codes are automatically flagged as "Insufficient Evidence".
- Uploaded invoices lacking hospital letterheads are categorized as "Needs Review" due to incomplete source validation.
