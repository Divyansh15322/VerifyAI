1. INTRODUCTION & SCOPE
This document defines criteria for validating notarized deeds, signed contracts, legal agreements, and witness attestation documents.

2. CONTRACT EXECUTION CHECKLIST
For a contract or legal deed to be verified as "Supported":
- Authorized Signatories: All parties named in the execution block must have physically or digitally signed. Missing signatures flag as "Insufficient Evidence".
- Notary Public Seal: Agreements requiring notarization must display the Notary Public seal and registration number (e.g., Notary Reg #NP-XXXXX).
- Execution Date: The date of signing must be present and within the validity window declared in the contract terms.
- Witness Attestation: Two independent witnesses must co-sign the document on separate lines or witness blocks, with printed names and identification numbers.

3. VALIDITY & JURISDICTION
- Governing Law: The contract must declare a jurisdiction clause (e.g., "This agreement is governed by the laws of [State/Country]").
- Stamp Duty: Agreements above a specified monetary threshold require government stamp duty seals.

4. EXCLUSIONS
- Unsigned signature blocks default the document to "Insufficient Evidence".
- Undated witness signatures are flagged as "Needs Review".
